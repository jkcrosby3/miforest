Comparing Healthcare and Educational Spending to Military Spending (2013)

Relationships and Trends

  • United States and European Union rank top, with the US higher in education and EU higher in healthcare spending. USA spending on military clearly outranks everyone.
  • It is difficult to see where everyone else stands, as they are at the bottom of the chart. The US and EU would need to be removed to see what is really happening.

Comparing Healthcare and Educational Spending to Military Spending (2013) (excluding USA and EU)

Relationships and Trends

  • It should be noted that China, Russia, Korea, Saudi Arabia, and Canada all had no data for Education Spending. To ensure they were included, a 0 was given to them for Education.
  • The largest military spender of this group is clearly China, as well as in Healthcare spending. If they had data for Education spending, China would clearly lead this group in all three categories.
  • Russia and Saudi Arabia are the next two largest Military spenders, however their Healthcare spending is incredibly low. If Education spending follows suit and is low as well, that doesn't bode well for their people.

Comparing per person Military Spending to the per person GDP (2015)

GDP per person (blue) | Military Spending per Person (red)

Relationships and Trends

  • United States and Saudi Arabia clearly stand out when comparing Military spending per person to GDP per person, with Saudi Arabia's Military expenditure being larger than the United States. That says a lot, given that the USA has the largest monetary expenditure towards the Military, yet on a per person basis, Saudi Arabia outspends on the military almost $1000 more per person.
