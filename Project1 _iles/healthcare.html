Analysis of the Healthcare Spending of the G20 Countries

Compare the per person healthcare spending to the per person GDP

Observations

  • Looking at the blue bars, or GDP per Capita, we see that Australia has the largest GDP per Capita, with the United States and Canada following close behind.
  • When we look at the red bars, or Healthcare Spending per Capita, we see that the USA has the highest values, and Australia and Canada have the second and third highest respectively.
  • India looks to have the smallest healthcare comparison to GDP.

Fastest growing countries in healthcare spending in fixed value (2009-2015)

Observations

  • When comparing across the years China takes the lead in healthcare expenditure, then India, with the EU third, but a little less than 1/3 of China's expenditure.
  • The USA is 4th with about 1/4 of China's expenditure on healthcare.
  • It appears that only the top 6 countries have an increase in healthcare expenditure, while the rest of the G20 countries seem to have plateaued.

Fastest growing countries in healthcare spending in percentage

Observations

  • Looking at 2013, China and India combined equal to more than half (54%) of the sum of the healthcare expenditure of the G20 countries.
