Educational Spending of the G20 Countries

Note: There are quite a few bits of missing data for Educational Spending for the various countries. If data was missing, a 0 was inserted to insure the country would show up on the charts.

Compare the per person educational spending to the per person GDP

Observations

  • Looking at the blue bars, or GDP per Capita, we see that Australia has the largest GDP per Capita, with the United States and Canada following close behind.
  • When we look at the red bars, or Educational Spending per Capita, we see again Australia and the USA with the highest values, but the UK has the third highest, although their GDP per Capita comes in 5th.

Government expenditure on education, fixed value (2009-2015)

Observations

  • The USA, for the most part, has 6-12 times the expenditure on education than the other countries.
  • The EU is approximately 2/3 that of the USA educational expenditure.

Observations

  • Looking at just 2013, The United States spends 35% of the sum of the G20 expenditure on education.
  • The EU spends 23.6% of the sum of the G20 expenditure on education.
  • 5 countries don't show because their value is 0 because we have no data.

Government expenditure on education, total (% of government expenditure)

Observations

  • The top 5 countries in regards to population are: 1. China, 2. India, 3. EU, 4. USA, 5. Indonesia.
